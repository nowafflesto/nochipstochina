Take Action

What You Can Do Right Now

This isn't a spectator sport. Export controls are shaped by political will, and political will is shaped by public pressure. Here's how to make yours count.

01

Contact Your Representatives

Members of Congress need to hear that their constituents care about this issue. A short, direct message matters more than you think — especially on a topic that most voters aren't yet talking about. Call or write your senators and House representative. Tell them you support strict export controls on advanced semiconductors and chip-making equipment to China, and that you oppose any rollback of existing restrictions.

02

Share the Argument

Most people haven't thought about chip exports. That's not because they don't care — it's because the issue hasn't reached them yet. Share this site, share the Dario Amodei essay, share the key reporting. The more people who understand what's at stake, the harder it becomes for policymakers to look the other way.

03

Educate Yourself

The chip export debate touches semiconductor manufacturing, AI capabilities, geopolitics, and trade policy. The better you understand the landscape, the more effectively you can advocate. Start with the resources on our In the News page, and read our detailed case for why this matters.
